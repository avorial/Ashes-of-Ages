# Game 4

## Synopsis

Game 4 follows the consequences of Radomir's secrets. During the day, [[Istvan Vlaszy]] rides north to complete his courier obligation at a [[Remote Tzimisce Castle]], evading crusader forces in the countryside. After he delivers the package, red-capped Hussite war wagons attack the castle. His Tzimisce masters tell him the Tremere have manipulated or blood-bound some Hussite bands into striking Tzimisce holdings, then return him to Prague through wind-like magic.

Back in Prague, [[Dalibor]] has a dangerous hunt involving a regenerating rat-faced victim, [[Eliska von Vlasak]] buys strange fae-made cloth, and [[Father Zemislav]] witnesses a harsh Hussite burial-penance rite. The coterie then meets a Cappadocian churchman, probably [[Garinol]] or someone acting in his orbit, who offers rewards if they bury Radomir's crimes rather than expose them. The offer turns into a threat when he produces an ear said to belong to Istvan's sister.

The group goes to [[Mira Vlaszy's House]] and finds [[Mira Vlaszy]] alive. She explains that the ear had been given earlier as part of Radomir's Tremere arrangements and that she had delivered bodies to the [[Golden Lane Shop]]. Her testimony reframes Radomir's crimes: the Tremere trade may have fed a project meant to help [[Prince Josef Zvi]] resist antediluvian influence, though that does not absolve Radomir or the court.

At [[The Librarian's Library]], the coterie researches red caps, fae fabric, necromantic counters, and Prince Joseph's past. [[The Librarian]] explains that Joseph once created or contacted a protector for Prague, that the old prince tried to have Joseph killed by day, and that the current prince may listen if the coterie can show his agents are escalating the city toward war. A meeting is arranged in the [[Castle Catacombs]].
