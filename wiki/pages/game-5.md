# Game 5

## Synopsis

The coterie is escorted from the [[Night Market]] by [[Ari]], a wounded mortal servant of [[Prince Josef Zvi]], into the [[Jewish Quarter Hidden Yeshiva]]. Ari's aura looks unnaturally clear and he uses a hand-wave compulsion to pass the gate, suggesting possession or other supernatural alteration.

Before the masked Prince, the group reports that [[Jakub of Thessaloniki (Zakariya ibn Masud al-Muntazir)]] killed [[Radomir Pavlovich]]. They also ask about [[Garinol]]'s pressure to hide Radomir's crimes. The Prince refuses to vouch for every action of his agents, but acknowledges Garinol's status and usefulness in preserving court secrets.

Prince Josef redirects the coterie toward the Tremere. He asks them to investigate whether the Tremere's abominations are mere experiments, tools, or a true threat to Prague, and notes that other factions are also chasing the same leverage. He then takes [[Istvan Vlaszy]]'s right arm and performs a ritual that burns it down into tiny ruby-like crystals, exposing an ancient Tzimisce/Methuselah blood connection.

Afterward, the coterie debates the Prince's motives, Tremere power in Prague, Istvan's newly exposed connection to [[Shagra]], and whether the [[Fireflower and Proto-Vauldere]] offers a way out. [[Adrastos von Orlok]] is consulted about Tremere influence; he says the Tremere cannot openly rule Prague, but can make rulers bow by choosing whom to support.

The session ends on the rooftops, where Jakub introduces the coterie to [[Graziano de Veronese]]. Graziano presents the Fireflower as a path away from the Crown's curse and says Radomir was killed because he had begun investigating the Fireflower and the nature of blood bonds.
