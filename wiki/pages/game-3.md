# Game 3

## Synopsis

Game 3 resolves the disaster at [[Sabina Premysl's Cottage]]. The gargoyle smashes through the roof and seizes [[Sabina Premysl]] while the coterie tries to keep the ritual, the cottage, and themselves from collapsing. [[Jardani Jovonovich]] forces the gargoyle's wings apart, [[Theodosia Byzantios]] deadens its passions, [[Father Zemislav]] calls abyssal arms, and [[Istvan Vlaszy]] ultimately destroys the creature by taking its head.

The victory does not save Sabina. The ritual and the uncontrolled darkness around it lift her through the broken roof, where a floating Cainite takes her north. The coterie suspects the [[Tremere-Tzimisce War|Tremere]], but the transcript leaves the identity of her abductor uncertain.

An answering shadow-thing remains long enough to answer questions. It says [[Radomir Pavlovich]] was killed by "darkness" and by someone connected to "the ones who kill." The coterie connects this to the crusader-like crossbow bolt and to the wider chaos around Prague.

At Elysium, [[Dragan Premysl]] reveals that Radomir had secretly sold Cainites to the Tremere in exchange for war magic, texts, and secrets. The group also learns that a Tremere representative had sought retroactive permission for an Embrace, sharpening suspicion around Sabina's disappearance.

The crossbow trail leads to the [[Red Calf]], where [[Jakub of Thessaloniki (Zakariya ibn Masud al-Muntazir)]] appears as "Jakub" and speaks as a liberator rather than a simple assassin. He frames the blood bond as the chain of the Crown/Crone and describes the [[Fireflower and Proto-Vauldere|Fireflower]] as a way to loosen or redirect those chains.
