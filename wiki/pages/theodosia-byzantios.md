## Appearance

A young woman of obviously Mediterranean ancestry - dark hair, large, dark, unblinking eyes, and pale olive skin. She dresses as simply but nicely as she can, not anything of the noble class, more of what passes for the middle class in the medieval period. She wears a cross at her throat and a medallion for Saint Theodosia.

## Background

**Theodosia History**

As Theodosia's surname indicated, her roots are deeply tied to the fortunes of Constantinople, the great imperial city of the East. Her ancestors were not great nobles or generals, however, they were the artisans who made the jewel of the Roman Empire shine. For generations, they had worked in royal palaces and grand churches, funded by the gifts of the nobility seeking to show off the prestige and wealth of the only true city in Europe at the time. It meant that Theodosia grew up in relative comfort compared to many but with the anonymity of being nothing more than a well-off artisan's daughter.

She was still a young girl when the Frankish brutes calling themselves Crusaders arrived in her city. Most thought they were there to pass on to the lands of Jerusalem further to the south, and none knew the thieves in Venice contracted them to rape and plunder a vulnerable Constantinople. Theodosia and her family hid as the devils ransacked the city, carrying off its treasures to the hated Doge. She has never forgotten the sound of her father weeping as they stood in the _Hagia Theodosia, _the very church her grandfather and great-grandfather had worked to make so beautiful, stripped of its glory by those who violated that sacred place. Whether it was the stress and terror of those horrible days, or the anguish of seeing the work of generations of her family destroyed, it was that moment she had her first vision, of Saint Theodosia, for whom she was named, shining brightly upon the city that she held as one of her sacred places, restoring it with as a gift to God. When she awoke, she had sketched out the sacred image of the blessed saint upon one of the ruined walls, much to the astonishment of her father and the priests with them. It was determined that what Theodosia experienced was a divine gift and that her patron saint touched her to see into the divine world, and given visions of God and his saints, which she dually painted into icons. She never questioned such visions, as she understood that God was unknowable and that the mind of the divine was not for mortals to understand. Her visions were mere glimpses of God's divine plan, pieces that only she could see and translate into something that other human beings could understand. She was merely a vessel by which such divine communications could be shared and disseminated in an understandable and digestible form for those who sought God, and her place was never to attempt to understand God's purpose in them. For many years, Theodosia created such icons, seeing it as her sacred duty to help rebuild what was lost, tapping into such visions to help restore the lost glory of God's kingdom on earth.

Theodosia gained a reputation among sacred quarters of the city, and while some urged her to dedicate her gift to God in sacred orders, Theodosia never sought the monastic life. She felt her purpose was to do what she could to restore the glory of Constantinople and not the spiritual life. Her visions, however, attracted the attention of a monk all the same. Yahyeh came to her to speak about what she saw of the visions that guided her hand to create the sacred images, and the pair had many long conversations about it. Given the sacred vows he took, Theodosia never questioned the fact that she only ever saw him in the darkness of night, nor the fact that he seemed strangely obsessed with her gift. It was only when he decided to pass on his dark powers to her that she came to fully understand the madness of Yahyeh, but by then, it was too late, she was now drawn into that same madness.

Theodosia had gained some notoriety in Constantinople for her gifts, which was why Yahya had been allowed to bring her across into the ranks of the undead, granted permission by the divine patriarch, Michael. Theodosia's sire promised the ancient and powerful Toreador that Theodosia would be a perfect tool to use in the furthering of Michael's Dream, the vision he had for the utopia he was continuously shaping Constantinople into. As someone whose who family had been shaped by Michael's vision, Theodosia readily agreed, seeing in Michael's Dream a hint of the greater possibilities of which she had only glimpsed briefly as a mortal. She threw herself into the effort, eager to aid the patriarch. But her sire, a restless soul, was seeking something else. He began to wander again, and eventually left Constantinople all together decades ago, in search of new religious perspectives by which to understand the questions that troubled him. Theodosia was left behind in the city, with the patriarch and his madness, attempting to see the road that lay ahead for Michael's Dream. But increasingly her visions simply led nowhere, or to darkness, and incensed, Michael lost all patience with her and banished her from her beloved home. For the first time in two centuries, she turned her back on the shining city, into an uncertain future.

Since she last saw him, her sire has settled in Prague, with its religious fervor, and settled into his madness. He has begun to recall all of his childer to him, convinced that he nearly has the pieces of what he's been searching for, that God is speaking directly to him. While others of her kin are circumspect in answering his call, Theodosia, left without a home and with nowhere else to go, has decided to follow his summons. She does this with great trepidation as she fears what Yahyeh is attempting to do. Theodosia does not believe that one should attempt to read into divine prophecy, as humans - mortal or otherwise - are creations, unable to fully comprehend the full scope of the divine. Her sire is playing with holy fire, and it worries Theodosia, even as she makes her way to Prague to do his bidding.

## Disciplines and Powers

- **○ Seeing the Unseen** (0); V20 142
  **&bull; Obfuscate:** When a vampire tries to use her heightened perceptions to notice a Kindred hidden with Obfuscate, she detects the subject's presence if her Auspex rating is higher than his Obfuscate, and she succeeds at a Perception + Awareness roll (difficulty equals 7 minus the number of dots by which her Auspex exceeds his Obfuscate). Conversely, if the target's Obfuscate outranks her Auspex, he remains undiscovered. If the two ratings are equal, both characters make a resisted roll of Perception + Awareness (Auspex user) against Manipulation + Subterfuge (Obfuscate user). The difficulty for both rolls is 7, and the character with the most successes wins.
  
  **&bull; Chimerstry: **Likewise, vampires with Auspex may seek to penetrate illusions created with Chimerstry. The Auspex-wielder must actively seek to pierce the illusion (i.e., the player must tell the Storyteller that his character is trying to detect an illusion). The Auspex-user and Chimerstry-wielder then compare relative ratings, per Obfuscate, above. The process is otherwise identical to piercing Obfuscate.
  
  **&bull; Other Powers: **Since the powers of beings like magi and wraiths function differently from vampiric Disciplines, a simple comparison of relative ratings isn't applicable. To keep things simple, both characters make a resisted roll. The vampire rolls Perception + Awareness, while the subject rolls Manipulation + Subterfuge. Again, the difficulty is 7, and the character with the most successes wins.
- **Auspex** (0); V20 134
  Auspex gives the vampire uncanny sensory abilities. She starts with the capacity to heighten her natural senses significantly, but as she grows in power, she can perceive psychic auras and read the thoughts of another being. Auspex can also pierce through mental illusions such as those created by Obfuscate -- see the sidebar "Seeing the Unseen" on p. 142 for more.
  
  However, a vampire with Auspex needs to be careful. Her increased sensory sensitivity can cause her to be drawn in by beautiful things or stunned by loud noises or pungent smells. Sudden or dynamic events can disorient an Auspex-using character unless her player makes a Willpower roll to block them out (difficulty of at least 4, although the more potent the source of distraction, the higher the difficulty). Failure overwhelms the character's senses, making her oblivious to her surroundings for a turn or two. While the Malkavians and Toreador are more prone to these kinds of distractions, the Tremere and Tzimisce aren't immune.
  
  Dots in Perception are very useful for using Auspex powers, as more successes help the character gain more sensory information.
- **● Heightened Senses** (1); V20 134
  It takes a reflexive action to activate this ability, but no roll or other cost is required. In certain circumstances, dice rolls associated with using the character's sense (such as Perception + Alertness) decrease in difficulty by a number equal to the character's Auspex rating when the power is engaged.
  
  The Storyteller may also use this power to see if the character perceives a threat. In this case, the Storyteller privately rolls the character's unmodified Auspex rating, applying whatever difficulty he feels best suits the circumstances. For example, sensing that a pistol is pointed at the back of the character's head may require a roll of difficulty 5, while the sudden realization that a rival for Primogen is planning her assassination may require a 9. Note that even this "precognition" comes only as a result of interpreting details the Kindred is able to notice. It's not an all-purpose insight or miraculous revelation.
  
  At the character's discretion, she may selectively heighten one specific sense, rather than leaving them all on. In these cases, the difficulty to perceive stimuli using that sense drops by one, but the difficulty to avoid distraction or temporary bedazzlement increases by one.
  
  This power does not let characters see in pitch darkness, as does Eyes of the Beast (p. 199), but it does reduce difficulty penalties to act in such darkness from +2 to +1, and the character may make ranged attacks in pitch darkness if she can hear, smell, or otherwise detect her foe.
- **Dementation**; V20 148
  Dementation is the Discipline that allows a vampire to focus and channel madness into the minds of those around him. Though it's the natural legacy of the Malkavians, practitioners of Dementation need not actually be mad to use the Discipline... but it helps.
  
  Disturbingly, Dementation doesn't actually make their victims mad, but rather it seems to break down the doors to the hidden darkness of the target's mind, releasing into the open whatever is found there. The Malkavians claim that this is because insanity is the next logical step in mental evolution, a transhumanist advancement of what modern people consider consciousness. Other Kindred scoff that this reasoning is an outright justification for the chaos that Dementation brings. They don't scoff too loudly, however, lest the Malkavian advance their consciousness next.

Increasing one's Appearance Trait has a difficulty of 9, and the vampire must spend an additional blood point for each dot of Appearance increased beyond their natural total.
