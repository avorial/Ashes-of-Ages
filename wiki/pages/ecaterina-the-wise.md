## Background

**Ecaterina, Brujah of Prague**

Embraced more than eight centuries ago, Ecaterina's origin is rooted in a violent encounter that shaped her indomitable will and thirst for justice--or vengeance, depending on perspective. A young woman of modest origins in Prague, she led a hard but fulfilling life until a pair of mercenaries, paid by the Ventrue to subdue potential dissidents, descended upon her. The attack left her disfigured and broken, her jaw shattered beyond repair as she resisted. Her world darkened into rage and despair, until Marhuel found her, sensing a kindred spirit in her unyielding will even as she lay near death.

Marhuel, her sire, was a Brujah philosopher and warrior whose ideals of justice extended beyond the individual to society at large. Upon Embracing her, Marhuel took it upon himself to restore not only her body but her spirit, crafting her into a weapon for their shared cause. Despite his efforts, the damage to her jaw could never fully heal, leaving her with a speech impediment and a permanent reminder of her trauma. This imperfection serves as a constant reminder of her Embrace and the cruelty that lurks beneath power--a lesson she has carried for centuries.

In the century that followed her Embrace, Ecaterina became Marhuel's fiercest childe, adopting his vision for justice in a brutal world. Her grand-sire Dominic, an elder Brujah with radical philosophies, also shaped her beliefs, instilling in her a sense of Brujah's ancient purpose: the drive to dismantle oppression, especially that embodied by the aristocratic Ventrue. She would later look to Dominic's Prometheans as her ideological family, embracing their vision of change, however violent the means.

**Life Among the Prometheans in Transylvania**

Ecaterina's allegiance brought her to Transylvania, where she joined the Prometheans in their long-standing struggle against the Ventrue and Tzimisce who sought to control the region. It was here that she truly grew into her role, honing her skills in battle and diplomacy alike. Though fierce and uncompromising, Ecaterina's disfigurement often led her enemies to underestimate her, a mistake few lived to repeat. Her work among the Prometheans ranged from enforcing their justice to aiding the oppressed villagers who suffered under tyrannical lords.

Over the decades, her scarred visage became a symbol among Transylvanian peasantry and Brujah alike--a representation of Brujah endurance and fearlessness. However, Ecaterina struggled with the Prometheans' methods, which sometimes bordered on chaos for its own sake. While she craved justice, she despised mindless violence and began to question whether her path lay with them. This conflict slowly seeded doubts about her allegiance, even as she remained committed to the Brujah ideals of freedom and defiance.

**Return to Prague and the Year 1433**

By 1433, Ecaterina had returned to Prague, a city simmering with tensions. As a Brujah elder with a reputation for surviving the unthinkable, she became a quiet force, influencing local Kindred and mortals alike. The memory of her near-death in Prague still haunted her, and returning to the city felt both like a reclaiming of her past and a confrontation with it. Now seasoned by centuries of conflict and disillusioned with empty rebellion, she began to seek something deeper--a way to use her rage and power not only to punish but to create change.

Her ambitions in Prague are twofold: to inspire the oppressed mortals and Kindred to fight for their freedom and to root out the deep-seated corruption, particularly targeting the influence of the Ventrue. She knows this means walking a thin line between diplomacy and outright war, a line she has often crossed. Over time, she has learned to balance her fiery nature with Marhuel's teachings, wielding her anger strategically rather than allowing it to consume her.

**Legacy and Philosophy**

Ecaterina's philosophy has evolved from simple revenge against the Ventrue to a broader vision of change. Although her jaw remains a testament to the pain inflicted upon her, she has learned to turn her trauma into a source of strength, inspiring others with her survival and resilience. Among Prague's Kindred, she is known as a fierce advocate for the lower classes and a relentless enemy of tyranny, with little tolerance for those who would exploit others.

Now, in the heart of Prague's Kindred society, Ecaterina gathers her own circle of followers, Kindred who share her vision of justice tempered with reason. Though she rarely speaks of it, she dreams of forming a new faction--a haven for those who seek meaningful change, who are not content with endless cycles of violence. In her eyes, such a haven would be a place where even the Brujah's rage is channeled, refined, and used to create something lasting.

In 1433, as Prague trembles on the edge of transformation, Ecaterina stands ready to be its catalyst, haunted by the past but relentless in her mission to shape the future.
