# Game 1

## Synopsis

In Prague in 1433, the coterie is drawn into Kindred politics when the Seneschal, [[Alessandro di Ravello]], summons them to [[Millie's Tavern]]. He presents the death of [[Radomir Pavlovich]], a powerful Tzimisce elder and city representative to the outer voivodes, as a crisis that could ignite another round of elder-killing and revolt. The coterie is ordered to investigate Radomir's haven and determine who could have destroyed him.

At [[Radomir Pavlovich's Haven]], the investigators find a property already being prepared for sale, signs of missing books and concealed ritual work, revenant blood, spirit-protective bedding, correspondence about the [[Tremere-Tzimisce War]], and a strange relic called the [[Stone of Hearts Wandering]]. They also recover or identify disturbing artifacts, including a fleshcrafted bronze vampire statue and notes about radical Hussite activity in [[South Market Square]]. Father Zemislav removes the statue under cover of darkness and takes it toward his chapel, while Dalibor arranges to purchase the haven through retainers.

The session closes with several active leads: Radomir's revenants, the missing ritual book, Tremere involvement, a radical Hussite meeting, a captured arson suspect, and the possibility that something demonic or spiritual reacted to the relics taken from the haven.

## Major events

- The campaign is framed around the Hussite crisis in Prague and a parallel Kindred conflict between elders and younger vampires.
- [[Alessandro di Ravello]] summons the coterie to [[Millie's Tavern]] and charges them with investigating [[Radomir Pavlovich]]'s destruction.
- The coterie learns that Radomir served as the Tzimisce representative in Prague and communicated with Tzimisce voivodes outside the city.
- Contacts identify [[Borislav Premysl]] as one of Radomir's revenant servants.
- The coterie searches [[Radomir Pavlovich's Haven]] and finds blood, occult markings, correspondence, missing books, and revenant-related clues.
- A document points toward a radical Hussite meeting in [[South Market Square]] at Vespers.
- Father Zemislav removes the [[Bronze Fleshcrafted Vampire Statue]] and is confronted by a demonic or abyssal manifestation while moving it.
- Dalibor arranges for his retainers to buy Radomir's former haven at auction.
- Eliska learns that [[Istvan Vlaszy]] was the second-highest bidder and that an arson suspect was taken to [[Castle Jail]].

## Clues and discoveries

- Radomir's haven contains ritual traces and signs that key texts were removed before or after the attack.
- The blood found in the haven is revenant blood, also ghouled by Radomir.
- Bedding with runes appears designed to resist spiritual possession.
- A concealed or chained book-space suggests a missing ritual book.
- Correspondence makes the Tremere an obvious suspect, but the transcript does not confirm they killed Radomir.
- The [[Stone of Hearts Wandering]] is a Tzimisce heirloom-like relic connecting distant childer to elder Tzimisce.
- Notes in the haven point to [[Marzanna]] and claims that burning the cultist-channel would strengthen winter and death.
- Crops in the region have grown harder to sustain for the last seven to ten years.
- A radical Hussite meeting is scheduled at [[South Market Square]] at Vespers.
