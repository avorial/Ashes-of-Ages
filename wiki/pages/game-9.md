# Game 9

## Session Synopsis

After the Tremere parley, the coterie debates whether to strike [[Garinol]] or [[Adrastos von Orlok]]. [[Istvan Vlaszy]] warns his caravan to prepare to leave Prague, then finds [[Borislav Premysl]] and [[Dragan Premysl]] at [[Dragan Premysl's House]]. Dragan is turning tied prisoners into [[Dragan's Szlachta Work|szlachta]] while claiming that spirits or true masters push his will.

[[Katherine Karálová]] researches [[Garinol Intelligence]], learning about his heretical monastic past, corpse-house interests, and inability to feed on the young. At the [[Night Market]], the group interferes with guards pursuing a young alchemist. Katherine later connects that thread to [[Rudolph the Clockwork Alchemist]].

The coterie follows Dragan's directions to the [[Hidden Salubri Cave]]. The Salubri agree to help against Orlok, but only with guarded trust and the logic of life-for-life. Below them, [[Theodosia Byzantios]] and [[Father Zemislav]] look into the [[Labyrinth Chasm]], a zero-shroud wound into the underworld. On the way out, the group finds [[Carrion Footprints at the River]].

## Major Events

- Istvan warns his caravan through the [[Gypsy Caravan Escape Warning]].
- Dragan reveals the route to the hidden Salubri.
- Katherine assembles [[Garinol Intelligence]].
- The group reaches the [[Hidden Salubri Cave]].
- Theodosia and Zemislav confront the [[Labyrinth Chasm]].
- Carrion-smelling footprints suggest a watcher followed the group from the river.

## Character Moments

- Istvan faces Borislav's accusation that he has abandoned his family and masters.
- Katherine acts as the group's scholar and investigator.
- Theodosia pays a spiritual cost for looking too deeply into the chasm.
- Zemislav functions as a dark guide through the underworld glimpse.
