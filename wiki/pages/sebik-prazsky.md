## Background

Seb&iacute;k Pražsk&yacute;, originally named Sadb, was born in Caledonia in 185 AD, part of a Celtic tribe deeply rooted in mysticism and nature worship. Before his embrace, he was known among his people as a faith healer, someone who communed with the spirits and offered solace to the sick and wounded. His life, however, took a drastic turn during a nighttime raid on a Roman legion encampment. It was during this raid that Sadb encountered a powerful Nosferatu elder, a Kindred who had been traveling with the Roman legions in search of ancient lore lost to their kind. The elder, impressed by Sadb's fearlessness and mystical knowledge, saw potential in him and decided to embrace him, transforming him into one of the undead. With the embrace, Sadb became Seb&iacute;k, shedding his mortal name and beginning his journey into the dark world of eternal life. After his embrace, Seb&iacute;k traveled back to Rome with his sire, who belonged to a sect of Nosferatu scholars devoted to recovering lost knowledge. In Rome, he was commissioned by the local Nosferatu clan to embark on a perilous mission: to gather forgotten lore and lost secrets from the farthest reaches of the world. This mission took Seb&iacute;k across continents, as he ventured through Persia, India, and eventually to the great city of Yanjing in China. There, he encountered the enigmatic Wan Kuei, the Eastern vampires, and managed to steal ancient scrolls containing powerful Yin magics. His journeys broadened his knowledge and understanding of the supernatural world, and he deposited many of these secrets with his childer in Prague, creating an early foundation for his hidden library. Yet, it wasn't until centuries later that the true cost of this knowledge would reveal itself. Returning to Prague in 1346, Seb&iacute;k was part of a caravan that brought the Black Plague to Europe. To his horror, he realized that the plague was not a mere mortal disease, but the result of a terrible curse tied to the scrolls of Yin magic he had taken from the Wan Kuei. The Yin energies had tainted the caravan's members and spread through the city like wildfire, leaving death and destruction in its wake. Realizing the dangerous nature of the knowledge he had uncovered, Seb&iacute;k became paranoid about the potential for this cursed knowledge to bring further calamities. It was then that he and his childer created the Giftschrank, a hidden repository beneath the University of Prague, where he would store all forbidden texts and artifacts that held the potential to unleash great harm on the world. Only Seb&iacute;k and his most trusted brood were permitted to access this vault, safeguarding humanity from the darker aspects of his discoveries. As the centuries passed, Seb&iacute;k adapted to life in Prague, and his influence grew in the shadows. His greatest accomplishment was his role in the creation of the University of Prague in 1348, one of the first universities in Central Europe. Seb&iacute;k had a direct hand in guiding the development of its curriculum, ensuring that the institution became a hub for intellectuals, scholars, and philosophers. By embedding himself in the academic elite, Seb&iacute;k could easily gather new knowledge while maintaining his secrecy. His role as "The Librarian" grew out of this era, as both mortals and Kindred who sought ancient wisdom often found themselves indebted to him for his unparalleled knowledge of history, theology, and the occult. In time, Seb&iacute;k's scholarly interests led him to discover Kl&aacute;ra Kar&aacute;lov&aacute;, a noblewoman with a thirst for knowledge who had disguised herself as a man to attend classes at the university. Seb&iacute;k admired her boldness and her disregard for societal norms, which mirrored his own insatiable curiosity for forbidden knowledge. When Kl&aacute;ra was discovered and relegated to the role of a maid, Seb&iacute;k continued to watch her nocturnal studies from the shadows. Impressed by her determination, Seb&iacute;k embraced her, believing that her intellect and ambition made her a worthy addition to his growing network of scholars. Together, they expanded the Giftschrank, turning it into a secretive library filled with arcane texts, artifacts of dark power, and knowledge that could tip the balance of power in the hands of those who understood it. Despite his vast collection of knowledge and his role as an elder Nosferatu, Seb&iacute;k's existence has been marked by a deep sense of paranoia. His experiences with cursed knowledge, especially the Black Plague and the Yin magic scrolls, have left him wary of what lies beyond the surface of his understanding. His brood, though loyal, have witnessed his increasing isolation and obsessive behavior regarding the Giftschrank's contents. Seb&iacute;k now rarely ventures beyond his subterranean sanctum beneath Prague, where he spends his nights studying the ancient texts, always searching for some hidden truth that has eluded him for centuries. His paranoia about the potential dangers of knowledge only deepens, driving him to lock away ever more dangerous secrets. By 1433, Prague had become a city of turmoil, with the rise of the Hussite movement and the schism between Catholics and reformists shaking the city's foundations. Seb&iacute;k has watched these developments from the shadows, recognizing that the city is teetering on the edge of chaos. While he cares little for the religious upheaval on a mortal level, he is acutely aware that such instability could lead to a power vacuum among the Kindred as well. More than ever, Seb&iacute;k believes that the knowledge he and his brood have hidden away is the key to surviving the storms ahead. ## Disciplines and Powers

- **Background: Status 2** (0)
- **Background: Generation 3** (0)
- **Background: Domain 2** (0)

- **The Silence of Death** (1)
  There is no roll. The power creates a zone of absolute silence around the character, with a maximum radius of 7 meters. No sound occurs inside this zone, though sounds originating outside the area of effect maybe heard by anyone in it. Additionally, if the player spends two blood points and the character spends five turns inconcentration in a room or enclosure, the silence remainsin it after the character leaves the room and lasts until she wills it to end. A character can't create more than one zone of silence, so if she wishes to use Silence of Death on herself (or curse another room), the zone in the first room fades automatically.
- **Unseen Presence** (2)
  There is no roll necessary to conjure an Unseen Presence. Should a Cainite draw attention to herself by sneak attack, blunder, or speech, accidental attention will call for a Wits + Stealth roll, difficulty from 5 to 9, based on the amount of noise or calamity the Cainite has caused. Willful attempts to violate the secrecy of Unseen Presence while still maintaining it may call for a high difficulty and multiple successes. For example, whispering to an enemy without giving away her location would require three or so successes at a difficulty of 7. Even when seen or unseen, a secondary use of this power allows a Cainite to conceal small items already on her person, or with a successful Manipulation + Subterfuge from 5 to 9, based on the amount of observation she can grasp and conceal an easily moved item.
- **Mask of a Thousand Faces** (3)
  To create this guise, the player rolls Manipulation + Performance at a difficulty 7. To mimic the face of another, the vampire must have a good look at them. The Storyteller might raise the difficulty on the roll if the vampire has only briefly seen the person they wish to mimic. How successful the disguise is depends on the chart at the top of the page. Should a vampire wish to create a face much more appealing than her own, she must spend a point of blood for every dot of difference from her Appearance to that of the disguise. While fooling acquaintances may require only a look at the target and clever crafting of the mask, fooling someone intimately connected to the target is another matter. That requires actual familiarity with the target, and perhaps other rolls to keep the illusion going.
  
  1 success  The vampire's mass remains the same. She cannot be taller or shorter or much bulkier, but she may change enough features so as to be hard to recognize (though she might be mistaken for a blood relative). A Nosferatu, for example, can appear as a perfectly believable, albeit tragically hideous human.
  
  2 successes  Her appearance is now a complete mask, and she resembles herself in no way, should she wish. She could not be mistaken for her own blood relative even in strong lantern light. She can now change how others perceive her gender.
  
  3 successes  Her intention is made complete, and she wears the mask she intends to wear. Her face is her own canvas.
  
  4 successes  The illusion is now so complete that the victims will imagine the vampire to sound exactly as the mask suggests. If it is a specific person, the illusion forces the victims to imagine the vampire is performing the correctt witches and physical mannerisms of the person they are mimicking.
  
  5 successes  The transformation is complete. Even age and size no longer hold the vampire back. She can make her victims  see her as anyone she wants, just by willing it so.
- **Vanish From The Mind’s Eye** (4)
  The player rolls her character's Intelligence + Stealth against their victim's Wits + Alertness. If there is more than one victim, use the highest pool to resist. If the player rolls one to three successes, the vampire fades but does not vanish, appearing ghost-like and translucent. With more than three successes, the vampire vanishes from her victim's senses entirely. If the player achieves more successes than the victim, the victim forgets that the vampire was there to begin with, and tends to not reflecton any conflict in memory that might cause.
  
  It is possible to interact with a ghosted vampire, though difficult. A player tracking such a vampire rolls Perception + Alertness at a difficulty of 8. On a successful roll, the character can interact normally with the ghostlike vampire, though they are likely to be disturbed by hisethereal presence. On a failure, the character loses two dice on any roll to interact with the ghostlike vampire, from attacking them to following them to seducing them.
  
  After witnessing a vampire vanish before their eyes, the player of any other character present must roll Wits + Courage. For humans, this is difficulty 9; for Cainites, difficulty 5. If the player succeeds, the character may act as expected in the situation. A failure on this roll means that the character is beside themselves and unable to act for two turns, unless that action is to flee.
- **Cloak The Gathering** (5)
  For every dot of Stealth the vampire has, she may add one additional person to her cloak. The cloak extends any single Obfuscate power the vampire already has to any character in the cloak. Large objects such as horses or houses may be added at the "cost" of an additional dot of Stealth. All members of the cloak must behave under the limitations of the power extended to them, and will drop out of the cloak should they breach the power's requirement. If the vampire creating the cloak fails to uphold the power's requirements, the whole cloak falls
- **Mask The Devil** (6)
  The vampire wills herself to become nothing, and her soul responds. Furthermore, because the Beast appears to be gone, any and all abilities, vampiric or otherwise, that might target the Beast such as the Animalism power Song of Serenity(see p. 192) fail, regardless of rolls.
- **Merit: Medium 2** (0)
  Ghosts, spirits, and shades speak to you. You may have had this talent in your mortal life,or it may be a new ability granted by your Embrace. By spending a moment in meditation, you may ask the spirits who visit you for their aid. They are ready and willing to help you in whatever way they can (most often by providing information), but they always ask a favor in return.The severity of this favor should be in line with the task given to the spirits
- **Merit: Lingua Franca 1** (0)
  You are able to speak a mixed language made of common tongues that most people you encounter will understand. You may not be able to hold philosophical discussions, but you can navigate your way through conversations in most places and will be able to communicate sufficiently in dire situations.
- **Merit: Literacy 1** (0)
  You are a learned scholar, able to both read and write. The Merit assumes that you can read and write with fluency in your native tongue, as well as Latin and any languages you may have gained through the Multi-Lingual Merit. In some chronicles, literacy can be assumed. In these cases, Illiteracy becomes a 1-point Flaw.
- **Merit: Multi-Lingual 2** (0)
  Through patience, study, and/or immersion, you are able to speak another language or languages with native fluency. This represents a full command over the language. For each dot of Intelligence your character possesses, you may know one other language. You may also use this Merit to reflect secret codes, cants, jargon, or sign language as well. Additional languages increase the cost of this Merit by one point per language.
- **Fortress of Silence** (7)
  Spend one blood point. Upon activation, this power inverts the effects of Silence of Death, simultaneously cutting a character off from external distractions while inwardly quieting peripheral subconscious background noise and chatter. The difficulty of all Perception-based rolls for characters meditating through Fortress of Silence is increased by two. This focused control over his own thoughts, however, allows him to reinvigorate his will, rejuvenating one lost Willpower point every 15 minutes.After a character ceases meditating and emerges from Fortress of Silence, the difficulty of all rolls involving Mental Attributes and Willpower is decreased by one for the remainder of the evening.
  
  To target an opponent with this power, the player spends one blood point and rolls Stamina + Occult (difficulty equal to the opponent's Willpower). The target acquires a Derangement if the character achieves more successes on the roll than the target has permanent Willpower. With 3 or more successes, this Derangement is permanent; otherwise it lasts for the duration of the night. For the duration of the scene, the amount of successes equal the number of Dexterity and Mental Attribute dice the target is prevented from accessing for any rolls. A target may spend Willpower on a one-for-one basis to regain dice lost through use of this power.
