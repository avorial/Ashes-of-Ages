## Background

Dalibor of Kozojedy

Dalibor is an exceptionally intelligent and highly driven individual with sociopathic tendencies.  Sly, manipulative, and charming, he is always three steps ahead of everyone else.  He is determined to keep himself a mystery.  As he puts it, "I'm a criminal.  Criminals are notorious liars.  Everything about me is a lie."  He dislikes rude people.

He has turned his back on his country because he somehow feels the country is responsible for the elimination of his family.  He functions with his own set of morals, that most of society wouldn't agree with.

He is extremely ruthless with no qualms about killing and often chuckles at a victims' death with a smile.  He doesn't necessarily kill for the pleasure of it nor is he a genocidal maniac hellbent on eliminating all crime, but rather sees it as an effective mens of gaining information, leading to the eventual deaths of his enemies.  He displays almost no empathy when killing people but instead covers it up with an affable facade to deceive his victims into lowering their guard so he can kill them or exploit them.

Dalibor is known to be extremely caring and compassionate towards his friends and is fiercely dedicated to protecting them and will go to extreme lengths to make sure they're safe, while also displaying a natural care for the members of his party.  He is polite, kind, and caring towards people he has known for an extended period of time, with his affable persona almost always on display.  He is nothing if not a man of his word, as he always keeps his promises.

He is very meticulous in his way of dressing, always making sure it fits the occasion.  He is usually found in highly tailored clothing, usually colorful, with a nice hat, expensive leather boots and a signature rose in the lapel.  Being he is also a master criminal, he is known to dress for those activities as well wearing very nondescript attire when necessary to blend into the environment.

 Dalibor is a criminal mastermind, wanted throughout Europe.  Born in Kozojedy to a noble family. Dalibor was known to have a difficult childhood with an implied abusive father who he's said was somewhat of a "disciplinarian" and a mother to afraid to stand up to him.  Dalibor attended university graduating at the top of his class and was groomed to be a military leader when he disappeared while coming home to visit his family in 1415.  While gone, he pursued a life of intrigue and crime, working for various royal families as a spy and an accomplished thief.  Dalibor has done favors for an unlimited number of influential people, and has a list of "favors" which he vows he'll call on them to repay in the future.

Dalibor didn't return home until he learned of his family's untimely death in 1428.  He spent the last three years imprisoned in Daliborka Tower, where he taught himself to play the violin.  He was imprisoned for assisting surfs in and around Prague helping them financially and adding them to his already extensive network. He has recently sought and gained immunity for all his crimes by working on behalf of the crown, and church working as a spy against the Hussite uprising.  He strongly believes the crown or the church had something to do with his parents demise.

Dalibor has a Tori "ally" named, Adrastos von Orlok, an 8th-generation Toreador, was embraced 1102 in the heart of Prague's noble circles. Born to an aristocratic family, he was known for his exceptional beauty and his patronage of the arts. His embrace came at the hands of a Toreador elder who admired his charm and cultural influence. This elder, seeing in Adrastos the perfect combination of ambition and refinement, brought him into the fold of the Toreador Clan, forever binding him to the immortal world. After his embrace, Adrastos quickly adapted to his new life, using his Toreador abilities to continue his rise in society, even as a vampire, building a powerful reputation among Prague's elite.

Residing within the Old Town district of Prague, Adrastos maintains a luxurious haven in a grand estate, surrounded by beauty and splendor. His home, filled with fine art, sculptures, and lavish decorations, is not only a reflection of his Toreador nature but also a place of political maneuvering. Nestled in the center of Prague's cultural and political hub, Adrastos is perfectly positioned to influence both the mortal and vampiric spheres of power. His estate is also where he secretly conducts his manipulations, using the shadows of its beautiful halls to control those who enter, all while playing the role of a cultured noble.

Beneath his elegant exterior lies a ruthless schemer who has been manipulating a small group of Salubri. Adrastos pretends to offer them protection from the Tremere, who hunt the Salubri to extinction. Using his Toreador charm, he convinces them they are safe under his wing. However, in secret, Adrastos enslaves them through the Blood Bond, using his vitae to ensure their loyalty while subtly breaking their will. He takes advantage of their trust, turning them into tools for his own advancement, all the while pretending to shield them from the Tremere's wrath.

The relationship between Adrastos and the Tremere is one of calculated balance. Publicly, he maintains a neutral or even friendly relationship with the clan, ensuring that his dealings with the Salubri remain hidden. He plays both sides of the conflict, using his knowledge of the Salubri's whereabouts to occasionally feed information to the Tremere, thus ensuring his continued survival and gaining small political favors. However, his true loyalty is to no one but himself. He understands that the Tremere's power in Prague is significant, but he views them as both a threat and a resource to be manipulated for his own gain.

Adrastos' ultimate goal is to dominate Prague's vampiric society and rise to greater power. He sees the Salubri as valuable pawns in this ambition--not only as leverage against the Tremere but also for their unique abilities. The ancient knowledge of the Salubri, particularly their healing Discipline, Valeren, fascinates Adrastos, and he uses it for his personal benefit. While he has no interest in the altruistic aspects of their powers, he seeks to harness their techniques for his own purposes, including the ability to dominate and manipulate others more effectively.

Despite his meticulous planning, Adrastos' manipulations have their dangers. His vanity as a Toreador makes him vulnerable to flattery and indulgence, and his constant betrayal of others breeds mistrust among his peers. Even those bound to him by blood are at risk of turning against him should they ever discover the truth of his schemes. This makes his position precarious, particularly if the Tremere or the Salubri realize they have been deceived. Adrastos thrives in the web of lies he has spun, but it is a delicate balance that could collapse with one wrong move.

Dalibor's sire is Wu Pinxiang, a 6th generation Salubri working throughout the Bohemian region.  He was very interested in Adrastos  von Orlov and his subversion of other Salubri in the area.  Wu continually tried to infiltrate Adrastos' clan but was unsuccessful for many decades.

Then Wu met a young Dalibor.  Wu saw potential in  Dalibor,  admired his stealth, his ability to build and use his connections at university to bolster his wealth.  Befriending fellow students of wealthy families and later relieving them of their most precious possessions, all while bringing some low level criminal to justice by planting some small object, while keeping the more expensive items and smuggling them out using the extensive network Dalibor built by doing favors for almost everyone he met.  Wu admired the strange code of honor and loyalty displayed by Dalibor, yet fearful of his protege's ruthlessness.

He watched Dalibor rescue a teenage boy, Demir Zeka a refugee from the crusades.  The boy nearly staved to death and was being bullied by older street urchins.  Dalibor had no issues slaying the boy's tormentors and taking him in, nursing him back to health and mentoring him.  Teaching him to read and write, educating him in the arts, martial and otherwise.

After watching Dalibor for several years Wu brought young Dalibor into the fold of the Salubri clan, and groomed him specifically to infiltrate Adrastos von Orlok's clan.

Demir Zeka grew up and became Dalibor's most trusted friend.  He serves as Dalibor's body guard, confidant, and oversees Dalibor's extensive network of smugglers, thieves, and spies.  Demir owes his life to Dalibor and their bond strong.

To use a bite attack, the vampire must first perform a successful clinch, hold, or tackle maneuver (see below). - **Obfuscate** (0); VDA 224
  To many common men and women living in the 13th century, the Devil lurks in every shadow and hides behind every corner. Evil is ever-present and a threat that can rarely be seen with your eyes.
  In the case of those Cainites who have mastered Obfuscate, this paranoia is justified. While evil may be debatable, Obfuscate makes it so that a gifted vampire could be lurking in the darkness ready to strike or flee at any time. The monster must be close at hand to accomplish this, no more than five meters per dots of Wits + Stealth from their would-be victims.
  Most mortals, even those trained in Awareness, cannot pierce the monster's deception. Some unique individuals may be able to see straight through these lies; children, horses, dogs, some cats, and very few religious visionaries are unaffected by Obfuscate, but this is all at Storyteller discretion.
  Cainite with the Discipline of Auspex may be able to pierce a Cainite's Obfuscate. For more, see Auspex on p. 195.
  Most Obfuscate powers last a scene once activated,unless willed away by the Cainite. They take no particular concentration to uphold once raised.
- **● The Silence of Death** (1); VDA 225
  There is no roll. The power creates a zone of absolute silence around the character, with a maximum radius of 7 meters. No sound occurs inside this zone, though sounds originating outside the area of effect may be heard by anyone in it. Additionally, if the player spends two blood points and the character spends five turns in concentration in a room or enclosure, the silence remains in it after the character leaves the room and lasts until she wills it to end. A character can't create more than one zone of silence, so if she wishes to use Silence of Death on herself (or curse another room), the zone in the first room fades automatically.
- **●● Unseen Presence** (2); VDA 225
  There is no roll necessary to conjure an Unseen Presence. Should a Cainite draw attention to herself by sneak attack, blunder, or speech, accidental attention will call for a Wits + Stealth roll, difficulty from 5 to 9, based on the amount of noise or calamity the Cainite has caused. Willful attempts to violate the secrecy of Unseen Presence while still maintaining it may call for a high difficulty and multiple successes. For example, whispering to an enemy without giving away her location would require three or so successes at a difficulty of 7.
  Even when seen or unseen, a secondary use of this power allows a Cainite to conceal small items already on her person, or with a successful Manipulation + Subterfuge from 5 to 9, based on the amount of observation she can grasp and conceal an easily moved item.

- **Auspex** (0); V20 134
  Auspex gives the vampire uncanny sensory abilities. She starts with the capacity to heighten her natural senses significantly, but as she grows in power, she can perceive psychic auras and read the thoughts of another being. Auspex can also pierce through mental illusions such as those created by Obfuscate -- see the sidebar "Seeing the Unseen" on p. 142 for more.
  
  However, a vampire with Auspex needs to be careful. Her increased sensory sensitivity can cause her to be drawn in by beautiful things or stunned by loud noises or pungent smells. Sudden or dynamic events can disorient an Auspex-using character unless her player makes a Willpower roll to block them out (difficulty of at least 4, although the more potent the source of distraction, the higher the difficulty). Failure overwhelms the character's senses, making her oblivious to her surroundings for a turn or two. While the Malkavians and Toreador are more prone to these kinds of distractions, the Tremere and Tzimisce aren't immune.
  
  Dots in Perception are very useful for using Auspex powers, as more successes help the character gain more sensory information.
- **● Heightened Senses** (1); V20 134
  It takes a reflexive action to activate this ability, but no roll or other cost is required. In certain circumstances, dice rolls associated with using the character's sense (such as Perception + Alertness) decrease in difficulty by a number equal to the character's Auspex rating when the power is engaged.
  
  The Storyteller may also use this power to see if the character perceives a threat. In this case, the Storyteller privately rolls the character's unmodified Auspex rating, applying whatever difficulty he feels best suits the circumstances. For example, sensing that a pistol is pointed at the back of the character's head may require a roll of difficulty 5, while the sudden realization that a rival for Primogen is planning her assassination may require a 9. Note that even this "precognition" comes only as a result of interpreting details the Kindred is able to notice. It's not an all-purpose insight or miraculous revelation.
  
  At the character's discretion, she may selectively heighten one specific sense, rather than leaving them all on. In these cases, the difficulty to perceive stimuli using that sense drops by one, but the difficulty to avoid distraction or temporary bedazzlement increases by one.
  
  This power does not let characters see in pitch darkness, as does Eyes of the Beast (p. 199), but it does reduce difficulty penalties to act in such darkness from +2 to +1, and the character may make ranged attacks in pitch darkness if she can hear, smell, or otherwise detect her foe.

A failure indicates that the play of colors and patterns yields no prevailing impression. A botch indicates a false or erroneous interpretation. The Storyteller may wish to make this roll, thus keeping the player in the dark as to the veracity of the character's interpretation.

Note that it is nearly impossible to determine with certainty if a particular character is lying or not with this power - vampires are inherently deceitful by nature, but even mortals might react with anxiety to questions while still being truthful. It is, however, helpful in determine the target's emotional state, which might lead the vampire to decide that a particular target is suspicious.
  
  A character may choose to perform a very cursory aura scan of a large area like a nightclub's dance floor or the audience in a gallery.   The Salubri instinctually realizes when she's in the presence of a character or event that is significant to her future (and the narrative, as determined by the Storyteller). Sense Cycle automatically informs the Salubri of potentially hostile encounters in the immediate future, ensuring she'll only be encountered unawares in locales unfamiliar to her. If she spends one Willpower point and rolls Perception + Enigmas or Empathy (difficulty 7), she may glean omens and visions of the person in question.   The character spends a blood point (which wells up under her fingertips), touches her victim (smearing the blood on them, possibly requiring a Stealth roll),then the player must roll Manipulation + Empathy (difficulty 7). Each success extends the power's duration for one evening. The difficulty of the target's Social rolls increases by one as he finds himself within a miasma of annoyance and suspicion. Even mundane or positive acts draw significant negative attention, inspiring jealousy and causing every flaw to be scrutinized.
- **Path or Journeys** (0)
  Path of Journeys
  
  What is the point of survival if you have only an eter- nity of playing it safe and no real stories to tell? Those on the Path of Journeys do not allow their unique condition to limit their existence to feeding and fighting. Instead, they see immortality as one endless adventure. Those who stay in one place, clinging to one way of existence and one faith might as well be dead. The vampire on Path of Journeys is an explorer who can't help but turn the next corner, open another door, or crest just one more hill.
  
  Most on the Path express it by abandoning one city, one region, and one culture after another in an endless quest to see what else is out there, and importantly, what else she can endure. Some vampires on this Path find smaller ways to express the endless journey. In ancient cities like Rome, for example, it's possible to spend centuries exploring the ancient tunnels and catacombs.
  
  Additional Ethics of Journeys:
  
  &bull; Stagnation is worse than death.
  
  &bull; Survival is a thing that must be tested, not simply accepted as important.
  
  &bull; The moment you are used to a place and its people, it is time to move on.
  
  Virtues: Conviction and Self-Control
